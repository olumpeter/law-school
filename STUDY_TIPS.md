# Study Tips

## 1. Learn through understanding, not memorization.

Don't ask:

> "What is this document called?"

Ask:

> "Why does this document exist?"

Then ask:

> "What legal problem is this document solving?"

---

## 2. Every legal document exists for a reason.

Whenever you come across a document in a court file, ask yourself:

- Why was this document created?
- Who needs it?
- What problem would arise if it did not exist?

---

## 3. Learn by active recall.

After reading a concept, stop and type:

> **My understanding:**

Explain it in your own words.

Don't worry about being technically correct at first. Understanding comes before perfect legal language.

---

## 4. Learn from real court files.

Whenever you get an opportunity at the Registry, observe how the concepts you are learning appear in actual court files.

Ask yourself:

- Where does this document appear?
- What document came before it?
- What document comes after it?
- Why is it in the file?

---

## 5. Think about fairness.

When learning Civil Procedure, don't start with the rules.

First ask:

> "What is the fair thing to do?"

Then learn the legal rule that gives effect to that principle.

---

## 6. Build understanding one document at a time.

Don't try to learn the entire court process at once.

Understand one document, why it exists, and how it connects to the next document in the life of a civil case.
