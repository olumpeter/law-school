# Environment and Land Court (ELC) Study Notes

## Preliminary Objection (P.O.)

### Principle

A Preliminary Objection (P.O.) must raise a **pure point of law**.

The court determines a P.O. based solely on:

- the pleadings; and
- the applicable law.

No evidence is required.

### Key Principle

> Evidence cannot be filed in respect of a Preliminary Objection.

If determining the objection requires:

- affidavits;
- documents;
- witness testimony; or
- any other evidence,

then it is **not** a proper Preliminary Objection.

### Practical Observation

If an advocate wishes to rely on evidence, the issue should ordinarily be raised by a **Notice of Motion** supported by a **Supporting Affidavit**, rather than by a Preliminary Objection.

---

## Notice of Motion

A Notice of Motion is an application asking the court to make a specific order during the course of proceedings.

Unlike a Preliminary Objection, it may be supported by:

- a Supporting Affidavit;
- annexures; and
- documentary evidence.

The court considers both the evidence and the law before making its decision.

---

## Why the Ruling Began with a Notice of Motion

Although the ruling begins with:

> "The Notice of Motion dated..."

this does **not** mean the suit was commenced by a Notice of Motion.

The ruling concerns an **interlocutory application** for an injunction, not the entire suit.

The likely sequence of events was:

1. Plaint filed (commencing the suit).
2. Verifying Affidavit filed.
3. List of Witnesses filed.
4. List of Documents filed.
5. Notice of Motion seeking a temporary injunction filed.
6. Certificate of Urgency filed.

The court first determines the urgent application before proceeding with the main suit.

---

## Certificate of Urgency

The Notice of Motion was filed **under a Certificate of Urgency**.

### Purpose

To persuade the court that the application requires immediate attention because waiting for the normal hearing process may cause injustice.

Examples include:

- imminent sale of disputed land;
- threatened eviction;
- ongoing trespass; or
- ongoing construction on disputed property.

The judge first decides whether the application is genuinely urgent before issuing directions or granting interim orders.

---

## Trial Bundle

### Purpose

A trial bundle is an organized collection of documents intended to be relied upon during the trial.

It helps:

- the judge prepare for the hearing;
- advocates refer to documents efficiently;
- witnesses identify documents; and
- the court maintain an orderly record.

### Important Principle

A document included in the trial bundle is **not automatically evidence**.

A document generally becomes evidence only after it is:

1. produced through a witness; and
2. admitted by the court as an exhibit.

---

## De Novo

**De novo** is Latin for **"afresh"** or **"from the beginning."**

When a court orders:

> "The matter shall stand de novo."

it means the case will be heard again from the beginning as though the earlier hearing had not taken place.

This may occur where:

- the judge has been transferred or retired;
- the previous proceedings are declared a nullity; or
- the interests of justice require a fresh hearing.

---

## Key Distinctions

### Preliminary Objection vs Notice of Motion

| Preliminary Objection            | Notice of Motion                |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Raises a pure point of law       | Requests a court order          |
| No evidence required             | May be supported by evidence    |
| Decided on pleadings and the law | Decided on evidence and the law |
| Cannot determine disputed facts  | May involve disputed facts      |

### Plaint vs Notice of Motion

| Plaint                                          | Notice of Motion                                |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Commences the suit                              | Makes an application within an existing suit    |
| Sets out the cause of action                    | Seeks interim or procedural relief              |
| Leads to the final determination of the dispute | Usually seeks temporary or interlocutory orders |

---

## Personal Understanding

- A Preliminary Objection should never require evidence.
- If evidence is required, the issue should generally be raised by a Notice of Motion supported by an affidavit.
- A Notice of Motion does not ordinarily commence a civil suit.
- A Certificate of Urgency requests the court to hear an application immediately.
- A trial bundle is an organizational tool, not evidence in itself.
- A document becomes evidence only after it is properly produced and admitted.
- A **de novo** hearing means the case starts afresh.
